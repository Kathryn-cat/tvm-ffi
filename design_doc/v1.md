# TVM-FFI Text Format: Printer/Parser Design

## Goal

`@tvm_ffi.dataclasses.py_class` already gives us the structural part of an
IR definition: fields, reflection, equality, and language interop.  The
missing piece is text format.

The goal of this design is:

1. Auto-generate a canonical printer/parser for most `@py_class` IR nodes.
2. Keep the core model small enough that TIRx- and Relax-scale dialects do
   not devolve into hook soup.
3. Leave a clean escape hatch for syntax that is truly surface-only or too
   irregular to derive from the final IR.

The most important decision in this revision is:

> Auto-generation targets a stable **canonical syntax** for the final IR.
> Optional sugar is layered on top as explicit parse-time codecs.

That boundary is what keeps the design simple while still covering the real
TVM surface area.

---

## Validation Against TVM

This design was validated against the existing TVM implementation in:

- `3rdparty/tvm/python/tvm/tirx/`
- `3rdparty/tvm/python/tvm/relax/`
- `3rdparty/tvm/python/tvm/script/parser/`
- `3rdparty/tvm/tests/python/tvmscript/`
- `3rdparty/tvm/tests/python/relax/`

The current TVM parser really does rely on:

- a dispatch-token stack in `parser/core/parser.py`
- `(token, ast_node_type)` dispatch in `parser/core/dispatch.py`
- per-dialect `bind_assign_value` chains in
  `parser/tirx/parser.py` and `parser/relax/parser.py`

That part of the earlier draft was directionally correct.

However, the TVM tests also expose requirements the earlier draft did not
model explicitly:

1. **TIR regions are clause-driven, not just body-driven.**
   `PrimFunc` and `SBlock` bodies contain ordered clauses such as
   `T.func_attr`, `T.match_buffer`, `T.reads`, `T.writes`,
   `T.sblock_attr`, `T.sblock_alloc_buffer`, and `with T.init():`.

2. **Relax bodies are not just statement lists.**
   `relax.Function`, `SeqExpr`, `DataflowBlock`, and `relax.If` mix
   bindings, scoped regions, exports (`R.output`), and terminal values.

3. **The same surface helper may participate in multiple syntactic
   positions.**
   `T.launch_thread(...)` appears as a binding, a bare statement, and a
   `with` context.  `T.grid(...)` participates in `for` and `with`.

4. **Some important syntax is parse-time only.**
   `T.meta_var`, `T.macro`, `R.emit_te`, and
   `T.comm_reducer(lambda ...)` are not ordinary final-IR nodes.

5. **Modules require multi-pass parsing.**
   `@I.ir_module` has module attrs/global infos, extern members, forward
   references, and then definitions.

6. **Canonical printing and sugar parsing are different jobs.**
   TVM already normalizes to canonical forms in many places.  Trying to
   make one trait system both preserve every spelling and derive from final
   IR is where designs become patchwork.

## What Was Wrong With The Previous Draft

The previous draft had the right ambition, but the wrong center of
gravity.

### 1. The "role model" was too small in the wrong places

`Function`, `For`, `With`, `Assign`, `If`, and `While` are not enough.
TIRx and Relax need:

- clause-ordered regions
- module members
- expression-bodied regions (`SeqExpr`)
- exports (`R.output`)
- statement-like syntax for expression nodes (`relax.If`)

Those are first-class syntax forms, not corner cases.

### 2. `print_pre_hook` / `print_post_hook` were a smell

Those hooks were compensating for missing structure.  In TVM, `func_attr`,
`match_buffer`, `reads`, `writes`, block attrs, `init`, and `R.output`
are not arbitrary prologue/epilogue effects.  They are ordered clauses in
the canonical syntax of the owning region.

### 3. `DialectHandlers` were still a disguised dispatch table

Replacing token dispatch with `DialectHandlers` is only a partial win.
What matters is the current **region syntax**, not the dialect name.

Examples:

- a Relax function body accepts `return` and `if`
- a Relax dataflow block accepts `R.output`
- a TIR block accepts `T.reads`, `T.writes`, `with T.init():`
- a module class body accepts attrs, infos, externs, and defs

This is better modeled by the current region format than by open-ended
"dialect handlers".

### 4. Parse-time surface objects and final IR nodes were mixed together

`@R.function`, `T.grid`, `T.launch_thread`, `R.dataflow`, and
`T.meta_var` are parse-time surface objects.  They should not force the
final IR node format to carry parser-only responsibilities.

### 5. Canonical syntax and sugar were not separated

This is the most important missing boundary.

If the final IR does not retain enough information to reconstruct a
surface spelling, then auto-generated printing must not promise that it
will.

Examples:

- macro definitions and hygiene
- `emit_te`
- `meta_var`
- lambda bodies in `comm_reducer`
- completion/inference passes such as root-block insertion or access
  region synthesis

Those belong in sugar codecs or separate normalization passes, not in the
core auto-generated canonical printer.

---

## Design Principles

1. **Canonical first.**
   The trait system owns one canonical syntax per construct.

2. **Surface sugar is opt-in.**
   Alternative spellings are separate parse codecs.  They are never
   required for the canonical printer to work.

3. **Regions own syntax policy.**
   Clause order, scope rules, exports, and structural statements are
   defined by the current region format, not by a global dialect token.

4. **Final IR and parse-time helpers are separate layers.**
   Final IR nodes describe canonical printing.  Surface helper objects
   describe how parsed Python syntax builds those nodes.

5. **If the information is not in the final IR, auto-generation does not
   pretend otherwise.**

6. **Manual overrides remain available, but normal TIRx/Relax syntax must
   not require ad hoc hooks.**

## Core Model

The revised design has four pieces:

1. `FieldAdapter`
2. `NodeFormat`
3. `RegionFormat`
4. `SurfaceCodec`

### `FieldAdapter`

`FieldAdapter` is the only per-field conversion mechanism.

```python
FieldAdapter = ffi.text.field("name")
FieldAdapter = ffi.text.field("dtype").via(print=..., parse=...)
```

Responsibilities:

- map IR field value -> canonical syntax value for printing
- map parsed canonical syntax value -> IR constructor value

Non-responsibilities:

- scope mutation
- clause ordering
- context-sensitive parsing
- side effects

Those belong elsewhere.

### `NodeFormat`

`NodeFormat` describes the canonical syntax of one final IR node.

Important point: **syntax form is independent from IR class hierarchy**.

Examples from TVM:

- `tirx.SBlockRealize` is a statement node but prints as `with ...:`
- `relax.If` is an expression node but prints as a statement-like
  `if/else` region
- `relax.SeqExpr` is an expression node but prints as a list of bindings
  followed by a terminal expression

Therefore the format system should model syntax directly, not assume
`Expr` means expression syntax and `Stmt` means statement syntax.

Canonical forms we need are roughly:

- reference
- literal/call/operator/tuple/list/dict
- binding
- expression statement
- `for`
- `with`
- `while`
- `if`
- function
- class/module
- sequence-expression region

### `RegionFormat`

`RegionFormat` is the missing abstraction from the previous draft.

A region is not just `body + optional hooks`.  It is:

- a local scope
- an ordered list of canonical clauses
- optional nested regions
- export rules
- structural statement rules for that scope

```python
@dataclass(frozen=True)
class RegionFormat:
    clauses: list["Clause"]
    structural: dict[type[ast.AST], "StructuralRule"] = field(default_factory=dict)
    exports: "ExportPolicy | None" = None
```

The clause list is ordered and explicit.

This replaces:

- `print_pre_hook`
- `print_post_hook`
- `DialectHandlers`
- parser-side "scope={...}" questions

### `SurfaceCodec`

`SurfaceCodec` is the parse-time object returned by language-module
entries such as `R.function`, `T.grid`, `T.launch_thread`, or
`R.dataflow`.

It is intentionally **not** the final IR node itself.

Instead of a single `__ffi_text_parse__(parser, node)` that must inspect
AST node types at runtime, a surface codec exposes only the positions it
supports:

```python
class SurfaceCodec:
    def parse_bind(self, parser, node: ast.Assign | ast.AnnAssign): ...
    def parse_stmt(self, parser, node: ast.Expr): ...
    def parse_for(self, parser, node: ast.For): ...
    def parse_with(self, parser, node: ast.With): ...
    def parse_function(self, parser, node: ast.FunctionDef): ...
    def parse_class(self, parser, node: ast.ClassDef): ...
```

This is simpler than one giant `__ffi_text_parse__`, because:

- the parser already knows the syntactic position
- unsupported positions fail cleanly
- helpers like `launch_thread` can intentionally implement multiple
  positions
- auto-generated codecs are straightforward

### `SyntaxContext`

When a definition or region is entered, it pushes a `SyntaxContext`
derived from its `RegionFormat`.

The current context answers questions like:

- is `return` legal here?
- what does `if` construct in this scope?
- which statement callees are treated as region clauses?
- which clauses define names?
- which names are exported after the region closes?

This is the replacement for dialect-token stacks.

## Canonical Syntax vs Sugar

This boundary is mandatory.

### Auto-generated canonical support

The core system should auto-generate canonical syntax for things that are
recoverable from the final IR, including:

- ordinary expression nodes
- binding nodes
- loop / with / while / if nodes
- function and class/module definitions
- ordered region clauses such as:
  - `T.func_attr`
  - `T.match_buffer`
  - `T.reads`
  - `T.writes`
  - `T.sblock_attr`
  - `T.sblock_alloc_buffer`
  - `with T.init():`
  - `R.output`

### Separate sugar codecs

These should not be part of the core auto-derived canonical contract:

- `T.macro`
- `R.macro`
- `T.meta_var`
- `R.emit_te`
- lambda reconstruction for `T.comm_reducer`
- pretty spellings that depend on normalization history rather than final
  IR state
- completion/inference passes such as implicit root block insertion or
  synthesized access regions

These may still be supported, but as explicit parse-only codecs or
manual printers.

This keeps the core system honest.

## Region Clauses

The previous design needed hooks because it did not model region clauses.
They must be first-class.

We only need a small set of clause kinds:

```python
CallClause       # statement-like call derived from a field
BindClause       # assignment-like clause that defines name(s)
NodeClause       # a child node with its own NodeFormat
NodeListClause   # repeated child nodes
NestedRegion     # e.g. `with T.init():`
BodyClause       # ordinary body statements / tail expression
ExportClause     # e.g. `R.output(...)`
```

That is enough for the TVM cases we validated.

### Example: `tirx.PrimFunc`

Canonical syntax:

```python
@T.prim_func(...)
def f(...):
    T.func_attr({...})
    A = T.match_buffer(...)
    B = T.match_buffer(...)
    ...
    <body>
```

This is not "function body plus pre-hook".  It is:

```python
RegionFormat(
    clauses=[
        CallClause("T.func_attr", field="attrs", optional=True),
        BindClause("T.match_buffer", field="buffer_map", repeat=True),
        BodyClause(field="body"),
    ],
)
```

### Example: `tirx.SBlock`

Validated canonical clause order from the TIR tests:

1. iter-var bindings (`T.axis.*`)
2. `T.reads`
3. `T.writes`
4. `T.sblock_attr`
5. block-local `T.match_buffer`
6. block-local `T.sblock_alloc_buffer`
7. optional `with T.init():`
8. ordinary body

So the format is:

```python
RegionFormat(
    clauses=[
        NodeListClause(field="iter_vars"),
        CallClause("T.reads", field="reads"),
        CallClause("T.writes", field="writes"),
        CallClause("T.sblock_attr", field="annotations", repeat=True, optional=True),
        BindClause("T.match_buffer", field="match_buffers", repeat=True, optional=True),
        BindClause("T.sblock_alloc_buffer", field="alloc_buffers", repeat=True, optional=True),
        NestedRegion("T.init", field="init", optional=True),
        BodyClause(field="body"),
    ],
)
```

No hooks are needed.

### Example: `relax.DataflowBlock`

Canonical syntax:

```python
with R.dataflow():
    ...
    R.output(v0, v1, ...)
```

This is a region with an export clause:

```python
RegionFormat(
    clauses=[
        BodyClause(field="bindings"),
        ExportClause("R.output", field="outputs"),
    ],
    exports=ExportPolicy(from_field="outputs"),
)
```

Again, no dialect hook is needed.

## Parsing Model

The parser core stays small, but it now has a cleaner separation of
concerns.

### 1. Header-driven positions use `SurfaceCodec`

These positions first evaluate an expression and then dispatch on the
returned surface codec:

- function decorators
- class decorators
- `for ... in <expr>`
- `with <expr>`
- assignment RHS
- expression statements

This covers the places where TVM today relies on token dispatch plus
`isinstance` chains.

### 2. Structural statements use the current `SyntaxContext`

These do not have a header object to evaluate first:

- `if`
- `while`
- `return`
- `assert`

Their meaning is taken from the current region format.

That handles the TVM split correctly:

- TIR `if` allows static-boolean branching or IR `IfThenElse`
- Relax `if` requires `else` and constructs `relax.If`
- Relax `return` closes a function body
- TIR block bodies allow `assert` with TIR semantics

### 3. Clause statements are matched by the current region

A region context knows that, for example:

- `T.reads(...)` is a clause inside an `SBlock`
- `R.output(...)` is a clause inside a `DataflowBlock`
- `T.func_attr(...)` is a clause inside a `PrimFunc`

This is stricter and simpler than asking random values to parse random
AST nodes.

### 4. Modules use an explicit multi-pass parse plan

`@I.ir_module` is special, and the design should say so directly.

The canonical parse plan is:

1. parse module-level attrs / infos / extern-style members
2. declare globals for callable members
3. parse bodies with those declarations in scope

This should be part of `ModuleFormat`, not an undocumented special case.

### 5. Expression evaluation stays separate

The existing expression evaluator and operator overloading layer are still
needed.  This design does **not** try to replace Python expression
evaluation.

Decision:

- operator dispatch is a separate evaluator concern
- syntax traits may auto-generate convenience proxy methods/stubs
- but `NodeFormat`/`RegionFormat` do not own arithmetic semantics

This closes one of the earlier "open questions": it should not be mixed
into the core syntax trait system.

## Printing Model

Printing is intentionally simpler than parsing.

1. Look up the node's `NodeFormat`.
2. Print its header.
3. Enter the node's region formats, if any.
4. Emit region clauses in canonical order.
5. Recurse into child nodes.

Important consequence:

> The printer is a canonicalizer.

It does not try to preserve every historical spelling.  It prints the
stable syntax defined by the format.

This is exactly what we want for a generated printer.

### Manual override

For truly irregular syntax, manual override remains available:

- printer: `__ffi_text_print__`
- parser: custom `SurfaceCodec` / custom `NodeFormat`

But the normal TIRx/Relax cases above should not require manual code.

### Compatibility with existing `__ir_print__`

The current repo already recognizes `__ir_print__`.

Decision:

- generated printers should target the new name internally
- but `__ir_print__` must remain accepted as a compatibility alias during
  migration

There is no business value in forcing a flag day rename here.

## Mapping to TVM TIRx and Relax

### TIRx

Covered cleanly by the revised model:

- `PrimFunc`
- `For`, `While`, `IfThenElse`
- `BufferStore`, `Bind`, `Evaluate`
- `SBlockRealize` / `SBlock`
- block clauses: `reads`, `writes`, attrs, allocs, matches, init
- function clauses: attrs and param buffer bindings
- `launch_thread` via a surface codec that supports bind/stmt/with forms
- `assert` as a structural statement rule in the TIR region context

Explicitly not promised by auto-generated canonical printing:

- macro definitions / hygiene
- `meta_var`
- lambda reconstruction for `comm_reducer`
- inference/completion passes

### Relax

Covered cleanly by the revised model:

- `Function`
- `SeqExpr`
- `BindingBlock`
- `DataflowBlock`
- `VarBinding`
- `MatchCast`
- `relax.If`
- nested local functions
- module members including `ExternFunc`
- cross-dialect references such as `call_tir(...)`

Important detail:

`relax.If` and `SeqExpr` prove that syntax form must be independent from
`Expr`/`Stmt` inheritance.

### IRModule

The class/module format must handle:

- `@I.ir_module`
- module attrs
- global infos
- extern members
- forward declarations
- body parsing

This should be an explicit `ModuleFormat`, not hidden in decorators and
hooks.

## Example API Shape

The exact Python API can still be polished, but the important point is
the shape of the abstraction.

```python
Field = ffi.text.field


@ffi.text.format(
    ffi.text.function(
        decorator=ffi.text.decorator("T.prim_func"),
        name=Field("global_symbol"),
        params=Field("params"),
        body=ffi.text.region(
            clauses=[
                ffi.text.call_clause("T.func_attr", Field("attrs"), optional=True),
                ffi.text.bind_clause("T.match_buffer", Field("buffer_map"), repeat=True),
                ffi.text.body_clause(Field("body")),
            ],
            structural=ffi.text.structural(
                if_node=tirx.IfThenElse,
                while_node=tirx.While,
                assert_node=tirx.AssertStmt,
            ),
        ),
    )
)
class PrimFunc(Node):
    ...


@ffi.text.format(
    ffi.text.with_region(
        header=ffi.text.with_call("T.sblock", args=[Field("name_hint")]),
        clauses=[
            ffi.text.node_list(Field("iter_vars")),
            ffi.text.call_clause("T.reads", Field("reads")),
            ffi.text.call_clause("T.writes", Field("writes")),
            ffi.text.call_clause("T.sblock_attr", Field("annotations"), repeat=True, optional=True),
            ffi.text.bind_clause("T.match_buffer", Field("match_buffers"), repeat=True, optional=True),
            ffi.text.bind_clause("T.sblock_alloc_buffer", Field("alloc_buffers"), repeat=True, optional=True),
            ffi.text.optional_region("T.init", Field("init")),
            ffi.text.body_clause(Field("body")),
        ],
    )
)
class SBlock(Node):
    ...
```

The specific helper names do not matter.  The architecture does.

## Explicit Non-Goals

The core trait system is **not** responsible for:

- replacing Python expression evaluation
- preserving arbitrary source spelling choices
- reconstructing information absent from final IR
- implementing macro systems
- implementing completion/inference passes

Those can coexist with this design, but they are not the contract of the
auto-generated printer/parser.

---

## Final Design Summary

The simplified design is:

1. `NodeFormat` describes the canonical syntax of final IR nodes.
2. `RegionFormat` describes ordered region clauses, structural statement
   rules, and export behavior.
3. `SurfaceCodec` describes parse-time helpers returned by language-module
   entries.
4. `SyntaxContext` is pushed by the current region and replaces dialect
   token stacks and ad hoc handlers.
5. Canonical syntax is auto-generated.  Optional sugar is layered on top
   explicitly.

This keeps the system small, covers the real TIRx/Relax requirements, and
avoids turning "support weird syntax" into an unbounded collection of
hooks.
